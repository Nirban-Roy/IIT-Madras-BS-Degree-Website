STUDENT HOUSES & SOCIETIES

Get empowered, be part of our virtual campus, build real communities, and become future leaders.

Here in the IITM BS in Electronic Systems degree, students, faculty, and staff work together to support students and help them make the most of their opportunities.

Student Houses

All students in the program will be divided into 12 houses named after forests in India. Your house is where you seek, share, support, and study together— and do what you love through clubs and activities.

We invite the student community to feel at home in their forest houses: Bandipur, Corbett, Gir, Kanha, Kaziranga, Nallamala, Namdapha, Nilgiri, Pichavaram, Saranda, Sundarbans, and Wayanad.

Each house accommodates nearly 30 groups of around 20 students each, where group members are often from the same or neighbouring cities. House members are from across the country, fostering diversity and community spirit.

Upper House Council & Elections

Secretaries and Deputy Secretaries of all 12 houses form the Upper House Council that governs the houses. The Student Elections Committee, constituted by Student Affairs, conducts free and fair elections every August. Each house elects its Secretary and Deputy Secretary for a one-year term.

The house Secretary, in turn, conducts group-level elections, where group members elect their own Group Leader. All Secretaries and Group leaders are typically below 25 years of age.

Our Goals & Initiatives

We strive to create an inclusive, supportive, and dynamic ecosystem that nurtures student growth—both academically and personally. Here are some of our focus areas:

We nurture Leadership & Organizational skills

  • Students gain invaluable skills and hands-on experience throughout their BS program.
  • Opportunities to lead events, clubs, houses, and study groups are provided.
  • We encourage collaboration, team-based projects, and initiative-taking.

We enrich your experiences

  • From cultural clubs to technical hobby groups, there's room to explore and innovate beyond academics.
  • Students can propose and start new clubs that cater to unique interests, supported by the student community.
  • Social events, fests, workshops, and competitions further enhance learning and fun.

We promote equity

  • Gender, socio-economic status, or any other factor will never be a barrier.
  • Diversity training and cultural sensitization sessions are regularly conducted.
  • We support clubs & events that advocate for inclusion, fairness, and social change.

We care for your wellbeing

  • Balancing remote study, personal life, and work can be challenging— our support ecosystem stands ready to help.
  • Access to IITM's Wellness Community Center for counseling and professional guidance.
  • Your mental and emotional well-being is our priority; reach out if you need someone to talk to.

We redress your grievances

  • Any complaints (e.g., bullying, harassment) are taken seriously by the IITM BS team.
  • You may raise complaints about staff or fellow students’ behavior for resolution.
  • Email: students-grievance@study.iitm.ac.in

We hear you

  • Our student community is diverse, vibrant, and constantly evolving. We value your feedback and ideas for improvement.
  • Have suggestions to shape or modify policies? student-affairs@study.iitm.ac.in
  • Join us in building a supportive and forward-looking campus environment.
