Overall Structure

The program comprises three levels and a learner has to successfully complete all three levels to get the BS Degree from IIT Madras. There is flexibility to exit at any level. Depending on the courses completed and credits earned, the learner can receive a Foundation Certificate from IITM CODE, or a Diploma from IIT Madras, or the BS Degree in Electronic Systems from IIT Madras.

Credits & Duration per level:

  • Foundation Level: 43 credits | 9 Theory + 5 Labs
  • Diploma Level: 43 credits | 9 Theory + 3 Labs + 1 Project
  • BS Degree Level: 56 credits | 12 Theory + 1 Lab + Apprenticeship (optional)

The completion time for the entire BS degree ranges from 4 - 8 years based on the learner’s pace. Each semester has about 12 weeks of coursework, along with in-person invigilated quizzes and End Semester Exams.

Course Registrations

In each term, a learner may register for a maximum of four theory courses (depending on the time they can dedicate to the program). Full-time working professionals and students pursuing other academic programs can opt to take fewer courses, such as 2 or 3 per term, for flexibility.

Online Courses & Assignments: Each course typically runs for 12 weeks with 2-3 hours of video lectures per week, supported by practice questions, text transcripts, and graded assignments. All study materials are provided online.

Assessments

There are three main types of assessments for each course:

  • Weekly Assignments (online)
  • Monthly in-person Quizzes
  • In-person End Term Exam

Some courses also include programming exams, mini projects, vivas, or take-home assignments. Several courses have associated lab sessions conducted at the IIT Madras campus each semester. Some lab sessions may be online or handled through kits that students can purchase.

Exam Cities

The in-person Invigilated Quizzes and End Semester Exams are held across multiple cities in India. Exams are also conducted in centers abroad, such as UAE, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Singapore, and Sri Lanka (where there is sufficient number of students).

Learners outside of these regions may be allowed to take up remote proctored exams. An additional exam fee may apply for all learners opting to write exams outside India. If you reside outside India and do not find a center in your city/country, please write to es@study.iitm.ac.in for assistance.

Foundation Level

The Foundation Level comprises 43 credits: 9 Theory Courses + 5 Lab Courses. It can be completed in 1 - 3 years, with approximately 10 hours of study per course per week.

Requirements for Registration:
Without JEE: Apply for and clear the Qualifier Exam.
With JEE: A student who qualified for JEE Advanced 2023 is automatically eligible for admission into the Foundation Level of the BS program.

On completing the 10 Foundation courses successfully, learners can:

  • Exit: with a Foundational Certificate from IITM CODE
  • Proceed to next level: join the Diploma Level

Sample Courses in Foundation:

  • HS1101: English I
  • MA1101: Math for Electronics I
  • HS1102: English II
  • EE1101: Electronic Systems Thinking and Circuits
  • CS1101: Introduction to C Programming
  • CS1102: Introduction to Linux and Programming

Diploma Level

The Diploma Level comprises 43 credits: 9 Theory Courses + 3 Labs + 1 Project. Students must have successfully completed all Foundation Level requirements (86 credits in total) to register for Diploma Level courses.

After completing the Diploma Level, learners have two options:

  • Option 1: Proceed to the BS Degree Level (if Foundation + Diploma requirements are completed)
  • Option 2: Exit with a Diploma in Electronic Systems from IIT Madras

Sample Courses in Diploma:

  • MA2101: Math for Electronics II
  • EE2101: Signals and Systems
  • EE2102: Analog Electronic Systems
  • CS2102: Python Programming
  • EE3101: Digital Signal Processing

BS Degree Level

The BS degree level comprises 56 credits: 12 Theory Courses + 1 Laboratory + 1 optional Apprenticeship. Students should earn a total of 142 credits across all levels (Foundation + Diploma + BS) to be awarded the BS degree in Electronic Systems.

Core Courses at the Degree Level include:

  • EE4101: Embedded Linux and FPGAs
  • EE3105: Electromagnetic Fields and Transmission Lines
  • EE4102: Electronic Product Design
  • GN3001: Strategies for Professional Growth

Additionally, learners must choose 5 Department Electives (20 credits) from a curated list (e.g. Probability & Statistics, Communication Systems, IoT, Semiconductor Devices, Analog Circuits, Digital IC Design, etc.), and can also take up to 8 Open Electives (e.g. Operating Systems, DBMS, Machine Learning Foundation, Deep Learning, etc.) as part of non-CGPA or NPTEL courses.

Exit: Once the learner completes 142 credits and the course requirements, they will be awarded a BS Degree in Electronic Systems from IIT Madras.
